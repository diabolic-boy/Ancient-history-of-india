Historical Timeline

c. 7000-2500 BCE

Prehistoric India

7000 BCE: Early evidence of agricultural settlements in Mehrgarh (modern-day Pakistan).

3300-2600 BCE: The early Harappan period; development of small villages and towns in the Indus Valley.

c. 2500-1900 BCE

Indus Valley Civilization

2500-1900 BCE: The mature phase of the Indus Valley Civilization, with cities like Harappa, Mohenjo-Daro, and Dholavira.

1900 BCE: Decline of the Indus Valley Civilization due to environmental changes or invasions.

c. 1500-600 BCE

Vedic Period

1500 BCE: Arrival of the Indo-Aryans in northern India. Composition of the Rigveda, the earliest Vedic text.

1000 BCE: The late Vedic period begins; transition to settled agriculture and formation of early kingdoms.

800-600 BCE: Composition of later Vedic texts (Upanishads); early urbanization and the rise of the Mahajanapadas (16 great kingdoms).

c. 600-321 BCE

Mahajanapada Period

600 BCE: Rise of the Mahajanapadas, 16 powerful kingdoms in northern India, including Magadha, Kosala, and Vatsa.

563 BCE: Birth of Siddhartha Gautama (Buddha), founder of Buddhism.

540 BCE: Birth of Mahavira, the 24th Tirthankara of Jainism.

483 BCE: Death of Gautama Buddha; spread of Buddhism begins.

327-325 BCE: Alexander the Great invades northwestern India, leading to brief Greek influence.

c. 321-185 BCE

Maurya Empire

321 BCE: Chandragupta Maurya establishes the Maurya Empire, the first large-scale empire in India.

273-232 BCE: Reign of Emperor Ashoka, who expanded the Maurya Empire and later converted to Buddhism, promoting the spread of Buddhist teachings.

250 BCE: Ashoka's Edicts promoting Buddhism are carved across the Indian subcontinent.

c. 200 BCE-300 CE

Post-Mauryan Period and Early Classical Era

200 BCE: Fall of the Maurya Empire; rise of regional powers like the Shungas and Satavahanas.

180 BCE: Indo-Greek Kingdoms are established in the northwest after the fall of the Mauryas.

150 BCE: Establishment of the Kushan Empire by nomadic tribes from Central Asia.

1st century CE: Trade flourishes with the Roman Empire; early development of the Silk Road.

78-144 CE: Kanishka, the most notable Kushan emperor, who patronized Buddhism.

c. 320-550 CE

Gupta Empire

320 CE: Chandragupta I establishes the Gupta Empire, initiating the "Golden Age" of ancient India.

375-415 CE: Reign of Chandragupta II (Vikramaditya); great achievements in science, mathematics, art, and literature.

c. 500 CE: Composition of the "Aryabhatiya" by Aryabhata, significant advancements in mathematics and astronomy.

550 CE: Decline of the Gupta Empire due to internal conflicts and invasions by the Huns.

c. 550-750 CE

Post-Gupta and Early Medieval India

550-600 CE: Fragmentation of the Gupta Empire; rise of smaller regional kingdoms.

606-647 CE: Harsha Vardhana rules northern India, briefly uniting parts of the region.
